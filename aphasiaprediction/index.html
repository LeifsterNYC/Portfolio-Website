Broken speech,

Aphasia, often caused by stroke, can strip sentences down to fragments — the meaning intact, the grammar gone. This model reads the fragments and writes the sentence back.

or try:
restored

How it works

  1. 01

    Corrupt

    52,989 training pairs are made by breaking fluent sentences the way aphasia does — dropped function words, fillers, repetitions, swapped prepositions — while always preserving negation, so meaning never inverts.

  2. 02

    Train

    A flan‑t5‑base sequence‑to‑sequence model is fine‑tuned to map each fragment back to its source sentence, with the best checkpoint selected by BLEU on a leakage‑safe held‑out split.

  3. 03

    Restore

    On held‑out fragments the tuned model scores BLEU 47.0; the same model untuned scores 0.6. Essentially all of the repair behaviour comes from the fine‑tune.
